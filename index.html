Rise of The Roman Empire

The Roman Empire was the most extensive political structure in the ancient civilization. It got divided into Western and Eastern territory by the year 285 CE since it had grown too vast to be ruled by a single central government. It became an administrative nightmare.The Roman Empire first started by Augustus Caesar and became the first emperor. And it lasted until the last Emperor Romulus Augustulus was removed by the Germanic King Odoacer. However, the east continued as the Byzantine Empire until the invasion of the Ottoman Empire and the fall of the city of Constantinople in 1453 CE. The influence of the Roman Empire on western civilization was profound in its lasting contributions to virtually every aspect of western culture.

  • Government

    Democracies

    United States

    The United States established a form of Government. A republic that was inspired by the Ancient Rome's Model. In consequence, the founding fathers developed the idea of checks and balances from the Roman concept that affects the executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The Roman and United States systems both had an executive branch that is elected. The Roman legislative branches consisted of several branches, including a Senate that has since lent its name to the United States Senate. The idea of multiple legislative branches influenced what eventually becomes the Senate and House of Representatives as the two branches of legislation in the United States.

  • Architecture

    Structures

    United States

    The great buildings that house various branches of the American government are inspired by Roman architecture. Perhaps the most obvious example of this is seen in the Supreme Court. Cas Gilbert's design draws its inspiration from Roman temple buildings. The staircase, raised podium and the columns would not be out of place in the Roman Republic. Similarly, the white marble on the Supreme Court and throughout Washington, D.C., was consciously selected to mimic the architectural splendour of ancient Rome. Fluted Doric columns reflecting Roman architecture without any decorations at the top or base are present in many American buildings including The Federal Hall in New York City.

Even in commerce the practice of returning something that was purchased if it was defective was protected by roman laws and are still common in our current era. Also, let’s not forget the creation of apartments, technological advancements like toilets, locks, keys and even cosmetics. During their time on power. The Empire develop advances in fields like law, medicine, religion, politics and warfares that became the root of our current civilizations.
